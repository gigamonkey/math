Euclid's lemma
“If a prime $p$ divides the product $ab$ of two integers $a$ and $b$, then $p$ must divide at least one of those integers $a$ or $b$.”
$p$-adic valuation
“In number theory, the $p$-adic valuation or $p$-adic order of an integer $n$ is the exponent of the highest power of the prime number $p$ that divides $n$. It is denoted $\nu_p(n)$. Equivalently, $\nu_p(n)$ is the exponent to which $p$ appears in the prime factorization of $n$.”
Perfect squares
“In mathematics, a square number or perfect square is an integer that is the square of an integer;[1] in other words, it is the product of some integer with itself.”
Square-free integer
“In mathematics, a square-free integer (or squarefree integer) is an integer that is divisible by no square number other than 1.”
